Wasm Beyond the Browser
August 9th, 2019 @ UC Berkeley

Workshop Overview

WebAssembly is an emerging standard for sandboxing and running platform independent code. While seeing rapid adoption on the web, it's also finding many applications beyond the browser, from serverless platforms to IoT. This mini workshop provides a forum for discussing applications of Wasm beyond the browser, challenges to using Wasm in these domains, and potential language extensions to overcome these challenges.

Intended Audience

Our workshop brings together researchers, core WebAssembly developers, and developers exploring the implications of using WebAssembly beyond the browser. Our goal is to facilitate the exchange of ideas and seed future collaborations between these communities.

Format

The workshop will consist of a few short talks, breakout sessions, and a dinner. For those who can't attend in person, remote participation will supported through video conferencing.

Topics of interest (including, but not limited to)

  • Applications of Wasm outside the browser such as IoT, serverless, big data, library sandboxing, blockchain, etc.
  • Wasm runtime and libraries support for non-browser application domains
  • Interfacing Wasm with non-browser environments
  • Wasm language extensions for security, performance, etc.
  • Support for non-C-like languages in Wasm
